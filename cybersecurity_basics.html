Cybersecurity Basics

Cybersecurity involves protecting systems, and data from cyber threats. Common cyber threats include phishing and malware attacks, and social engineering. Understanding them is crucial for being safe online.

Phishing Attacks

Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers trick users into revealing personal information like passwords and credit card details. It often occurs via fake emails, fraudulent websites, or deceptive messages.

Types of Phishing:

  • Email Phishing: Fake emails pretending to be from legitimate sources.
  • Spear Phishing: Targeted phishing aimed at specific individuals.
  • Vishing: Phishing conducted through voice calls.
  • Smishing: Phishing attacks using SMS messages.

Malware

Malware is malicious software designed to harm or exploit devices and networks. It can be spread through infected downloads, email attachments, or compromised websites.

Types of Malware:

  • Spyware: Secretly collects user activity information.
  • Ransomware: Encrypts files and demands ransom for decryption.
  • Trojan Horse: Disguises itself as legitimate software to install malware.
  • Worms: Self-replicating malware that spreads across networks.

Social Engineering

Social engineering is the manipulation of people into divulging confidential information. Instead of exploiting software vulnerabilities, attackers exploit human psychology.

Common Social Engineering Attacks:

  • Impersonation: Attackers pretend to be trusted figures (e.g., IT support) to gain access to sensitive information.
  • Baiting: Attackers offer something tempting (e.g., a free download) that contains malware.
  • Pretexting: Scammers create a fabricated scenario to steal information.

Best Security Practices

  • Use strong and unique passwords.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA).
  • Avoid clicking on suspicious links.
  • Keep your software and antivirus updated.
  • Verify email senders before providing sensitive information.
